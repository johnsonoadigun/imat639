About Johnson Adigun

Johnson Adigun is currently a student at University of Maryland University College (UMUC) where he is pursuing a Master’s degree in Technology Management – Information Systems and Services. Born in Lagos, Nigeria, where he gained his Bachelor’s degree in Economics, Johnson moved to the USA in 2007. Since 2012, Johnson has held a position of an Assistant Teacher at the Ivymount School (Rockville, MD) where he works to enable students with learning and developmental disabilities make successful transition from school to adult life, with an emphasis on vocational skills and related work-life experience. Through certifications, internships and studies at the UMUC Johnson has gained various technical skills which he hopes to leverage for a new career path. These skills include: Ability to evaluate tactical and strategic benefits of data warehouses, data marts, and data centers; ability to describe their data volatility and decision-support characteristics and understand the process of building and using a data warehouse. Ability to describe the functions of database technology, the differences between centralized and distributed database architecture, how data quality impacts performance, and the role of a master reference file in creating accurate and consistent data across the enterprise. Ability to explain how sales and marketing information systems support advertising, market research, intelligence gathering, getting products and services to customers, and responding quickly and efficiently to customers' needs.
